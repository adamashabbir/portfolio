    How to crash a World Film Premiere & After Party - Spectre Special
    On Monday evening my best friend James Ware and I successfully crashed the Royal World Premiere of Spectre at the Royal Albert Hall. As you can imagine the occasion was unbelievable and unforgettable. Before you panic, I will not reveal anything about the film, except that the opening scene is a blast.

    The Spectre Royal World Premiere was probably the biggest event you could have been at on Monday evening and, depending on your general persuasion, that’d be closely followed by the after-party. The only problem is, is that the guest list for London’s Royal Albert Hall premiere was extremely exclusive. The only thing more exclusive would be tickets to the after party at The British Museum.

    So how do you crash it, James Bond Style?

    Believe in yourself.
    It comes down to confidence. There are too many sayings to list, but the confidence card is the first one to play. James and I believed 100% of the time we could do it and we did it. This also ensues you actually enjoy the occasion. Instead of panicking, stay cool, calm and collected.

    Saying howdy to Chris Evans

    Seflie with the songbird Sam Smith

    Make as much eye contact as you can with everyone. It helped hugely that James is a very charming and talented vlogger. His eye for attention led us to celebs including the song bird Sam Smith, Chris Evans and even the delightful Naomie Harris. This leads me on to my next point

    Blend in.
    For all you know everyone present believes you are supposed to be there so act like it. We took a sharpie for autographs, sunglasses and most importantly each had our black tie on, with self-tie dickey bow to guarantee we were not questioned upon arrival.

    Black tie & typical over the top sunglasses

    Black tie & a touch of tartan

    Arrive in style.
    Our arrival was also bond themed. Opting for an Uber lux, we looked the part and arrived in style. Importantly, know what the dress code is and go above and beyond to arrive fashionably late and with flair.

    Hack it
    There is even a hack for tickets. Many people oddly post them on social media. Search using relevant key words and you should find them fairly easily. As a developer we hack things together all the time; do your best to hack something together and hope it works. However, the important thing to remember is, our tickets barely got checked until we were in the venue. Therefore, you are at least guaranteed red carpet action.

    In is in
    Once you are in, have a gulp of something glacial. It will calm you down and cheer you up. Logistically, you have to be the last one to take your sit, so hang back and hold on. When you are ready, plough in and pop a squat where you can.

    Action
    Sit back, relax and let the good times roll. Enjoy the film and the spectacle. We were lucky to be joined by the royals. Comfortably in we took the opportunity to belt out the National Anthem, accompanied by a fanfare of trumpeters.

    National Anthem fanfare

    After Party
    Remember taking the time to talk to folks in the queue and on the red carpet? Well, as you know, certain people like talking about themselves and seem incapable of returning any questions. We didn’t even have to ask anyone if they were going or where it was. Three people openly boasted of going to the exclusive event and even took the time to show us their wristbands. We did our best with our Royal Spectre program to rip, wrap and stick together our very own gold wristbands.

    Fashionably late is great
    For the after party follow a similar routine to the film. Hop into the queue and be late. Maintain eye contact with everyone and smile more than a Cheshire cat. Ideally, distract whoever needs to check your wristband with a compliment. Once you are in, just revel in the moment.

    The rest is history
